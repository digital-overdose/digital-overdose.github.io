What's all of this about?

Digital Overdose is proud to announce that we are running a free online conference!

What will be spoken about? Information Security, Cybersecurity, OSINT, DevOps, Tech in general... Everything is on the table.

The catch? The only people able to speak there are rookies. Rookies not necessarily in the sense that they are new to Information Security, but more specifically in the sense that they've never had the experience of talking at a conference previously!

With the number of conventions with rookie tracks that have been cancelled, focusing specifically on rookies lets them have all of the well-deserved limelight, and helps foster the next generation of representatives of the community.

So if you're free on the 17th and 18th of April (2021), then save the date !

How much will it cost? / Attendance

The conference will be free for anyone to attend.

Approximately one month before the event, we will open an EventBrite where you can optionally acquire a ticket (for interest metrics only).

The stream of the conference will be publicly hosted on our YouTube channel, and if you miss a talk or a few, we will have the recordings up as fast as possible in the days following the event, with links to them on this page.

Are you interested? / CFP Submission

So we've managed to pique your interest? That's awesome!

We have some good news then!
From now until March 7th 23:59 GMT+1, you may submit a talk proposal to this address: digitaloverdosecon+cfp@gmail.com.

To make it nice and simple, we're thinking of 30 minute talks (25 minutes for the actual talk and 5 minutes for a Q&A).

And in the interest of making thinking about it as painless as possible, your proposal can be as simple as:

  • A title
  • The subject matter
  • A short description
  • Your name and twitter handle

We will then forward the first three elements (and nothing more) to a blind panel, to make sure that everyone gets a fair shot at speaking for the first time!

For potential sponsors

In order to provide our speakers with well-deserved conference "swag" and its shipping, organizations can choose to sponsor the event at a flat rate, in exchange for shoutouts during the conference and their logos appearing on the stream as well as potential wearable merchandise.

The use of this sponsorship money will be 100% to cover the costs of swag, and we will publish a detailed public notice for the sake transparency
